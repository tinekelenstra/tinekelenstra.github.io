Research Technician - Transcription Dynamics

NKI / Academic Transfer
More info

Your function within the department

We are looking for a motivated technician to join Dr Tineke Lenstra’s team. The main interest of the lab is to understand the regulatory mechanisms and dynamics of eukaryotic transcription. We use cutting-edge single-molecule microscopy techniques to visualize individual protein and RNA molecules in living and fixed cells, providing direct quantitative measurements of transcription and its regulatory steps. These imaging assays are combined with novel gene-specific targeting approaches to modulate transcription of endogenous genes. By studying transcription dynamics in single cells we aim to elucidate the molecular mechanisms of transcription regulation and to connect mechanistic insights to human disease relevance.

Your function

You will support a wide range of research activities in the laboratory, including microscopy, molecular biology, cloning experiments, and image analysis. You will oversee and support the development yeast and mammalian cell line models. You will also work closely with the PI to organise and coordinate aspects of lab management.

Scientific environment

The laboratory is part of the division of Gene Regulation at the Netherlands Cancer Institute (NKI-AVL) in Amsterdam. The research groups at the NKI-AVL are supported by state-of-the-art equipment and facilities, most with dedicated support staff. The Institute has a large mouse facility with special units for transgenesis, intervention studies, pathology and functional imaging, a genomics facility, a functional screening facility for RNAi and small-molecule libraries, excellent facilities for microscopy, flow cytometry, protein expression and purification, and many more.
